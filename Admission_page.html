ADMISSION POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

The Admission Committee of St. Paul School of Buug, Inc. accepts new students and transferees on the basis of the results of the entrance examinations, personal interviews, academic records, character evaluation, and the students’ and parents’ acceptance of the school’s philosophy, objectives and policies. Thus, the Committee is composed of the following: the Guidance Designate, the Student Affairs Coordinator, the GS/HS Coordinators and the Directress/Principal.

A. GENERAL ADMISSION POLICIES

· Everything is on a FIRST COME, FIRST SERVED basis. The school applies the Open Admission Selective Retention Policy. It means that everybody is accepted provided the students will meet the required grades for that grade level.
· Application for all levels starts in January.
· Test results are given by the Guidance Counselor/GS and/or HS Coordinator.
· Grades two to six and High School transferees must have at least satisfactory grades or its equivalent in any grading period and must have a satisfactory mark in conduct.

B. BASIC APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

1. Original and photocopy of the following documents:
· Birth Certificate (NSO Authenticated)
· Baptismal Certificate
· Parents' Marriage Certificate
2. Two 2 x 2 ID pictures (white background)
3. Accomplished recommendation form/Certificate of Good Moral Character (Transferee only)
4. Health certificate and immunization record coming from Public Health Center
5. Original Report Card with LRN
6. Certificate of Eligibility to Transfer or Honorable Dismissal (Transferees only)
7. Certification of Completion of Grade Level and Promotion to the Next Level (Transferees only)
8. Passing the qualifying test and interview
9. Age requirements per grade level:
Kindergarten 1- 4 years old (by June until August 30) Kindergarten 2- 5 years old (by June until August 30)
Grade 1- 6 years old (by June until August 30)

* Additional Requirements for Foreign Applicants:

a. Original and Photocopy of Passport (bio-page, admission, and authorized stay)
b. ACR - Alien Certificate Registration (ACR I-card)
c. Translated and authenticated birth certificate and school record from the Embassy of the Country they come from d. Grades with subject description (English translation)
e. Document and Certification of acquired Dual citizenship (if applicable)
f. Certification of Completion of Grade Level and Promotion to the Next Level g. Filipino and Araling Panlipunan as Prerequisite Subjects

D. ADMISSION PROCEDURES

New Students (including Transferees)

1. Submit complete requirements to the Guidance Center in a long brown envelope.
2. Pay the testing fee at the Finance Office.
3. Present the receipt to the Guidance Office and secure Testing Permit schedule and permit.
4. Comply with the testing schedule. Present the test permit to the Guidance Counselor on the scheduled day of the examination.
5. No test results will be mailed or released through the telephone, text messages, and/or social media.
Parents/Guardians have to come to the Guidance Office to inquire about the result.
6. Applicants who passed the entrance tests, together with their parents, will be scheduled for an interview by the
Admission Committee or its representatives.
7. The Principal makes the final decision on admission.
8. Qualified applicants may already enroll.

E. REGISTRATION AND ENROLLMENT PROCEDURE

1. Present Report Card for cancellation; secure and accomplish ENROLLMENT FORM/CONTRACT.
2. Proceed to the Finance Office and present cancelled Report Card and the accomplished ENROLLMENT FORM/CONTRACT signed by the parent/guardian for payment of enrollment fees.
3. Cashier issues an official receipt and gives the admission slip to the parent/child to be presented on the first day
of class. 4. Cashier forwards the copy of the cancelled card and the copy of the signed enrollment contract to the Guidance
Office. · All unpaid accounts of the past year are to be settled before enrolling for the current year.
· School fees are paid directly to the SPSB Finance Cashier only. · Books, uniforms and other school supplies are available at the Finance Office during enrollment. Only cash is accepted for payment for books, uniforms, and supplies.
5. A date is set for late enrollees. The school reserves the right to refuse late enrollees.
